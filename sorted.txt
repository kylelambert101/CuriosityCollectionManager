Text: # Black\-Margined Loosestrife Beetle
S: Galerucella calmariensis
Galerucella calmariensis is a species of leaf beetle in the family Chrysomelidae\. It is commonly known as the black\-margined loosestrife beetle and is native to Europe and Asia where both adults and larvae feed on purple loosestrife \(Lythrum salicaria\)\. It has been introduced in North America as a biological control agent for purple loosestrife\.

Text: Rover Ant
G: Brachymyrmex
F: Formidicae
So cool story: This tiny (~3mm) ant was on the table and when I started taking photos, I noticed that there was an even smaller ant (male) hidden behind the female in the process of copulating! To make things weirder, after the two detached, the female DROPPED HER WINGS and crawled away. 
Apparently this is standard for formicid ants like this - they fly somewhere to mate, and then the female sheds her wings before finding a place to lay her eggs and start a colony.
In this view, you can see the male more easily.
The male after detaching.

"This hybrid is very widely cultivated, being readily and inexpensively propagated by layering; as a result, it is often the commonest Tilia species in urban areas and along avenues and streets. It is not however the best species of this purpose, as it produces abundant stem sprouts, and also often hosts heavy aphid populations resulting in honeydew deposits on everything underneath the trees. Furthermore there is substantial leaf litter in autumn (fall)."
